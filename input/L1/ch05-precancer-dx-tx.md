## Section 5.1 — Cervical cancer screening tests (pp. 48–50)

A/641 lists **three screening tests**. Only HPV DNA and cytology are used in
the national screening programme; the acetic-acid (VIA) test is defined as
a procedure but not deployed as a national screening modality.

### 5.1.1 HPV DNA test

**Summary.** Primary screening test for women in HPV-capable units. Detects
14 high-risk HPV subtypes. Recommended only for women aged 30+, because most
HPV infections in younger women clear spontaneously.

> **English translation:**
>
> *Basic concept.* The HPV (Human Papillomavirus) test is the screening test
> most widely used for cervical cancer detection. HPV infection is highly
> prevalent among young women under 30, but in most cases clears spontaneously
> — therefore this test is recommended for women aged 30 and above. Of the
> many HPV subtypes, 14 subtypes that account for the highest cervical-cancer
> risk are designated as the high-risk panel for which the screening test
> identifies infection.
>
> *Who is screened?* Women aged **35 and 45**.
>
> *How is it performed?* In health-center units with the capacity to perform
> HPV testing. The provider first takes a cytology slide from the cervix,
> then takes the HPV sample. (See Op.Inst. 5.2 for procedural detail.)

### 5.1.2 Acetic acid test (VIA)

**Summary.** ⚠️ **L2-relevant correction.** A/641 explicitly defines this
test for women **under 50** (not just "transformation zone visible") and
**not for post-menopausal women** (TZ retracts into cervical canal). The
test is *not* used in the national screening programme but is documented
as a procedure for completeness.

> **English translation:**
>
> *Basic concept.* The acetic acid test is a method that uses a speculum,
> applies 3–5% acetic acid solution to the cervix, and observes for color
> change. The test requires trained providers and ongoing quality monitoring.
>
> *Who is screened?* Women under age 50 whose squamocolumnar junction (SCJ)
> is visible at the ectocervix. In post-menopausal women, the SCJ retracts
> into the cervical canal, making the acetic acid test inapplicable.
>
> *How is it performed?* Apply speculum, identify the squamocolumnar junction
> at the cervix, look for any suspicious lesion. Apply 3–5% acetic acid
> solution and observe for one minute. ⚠️ Pre-cancer (ХУЭ) is suspected when
> a pale (acetowhite) change persists for more than one minute. Pale changes
> from inflammation or physiological causes are quick and short-lived. Edge
> sharpness, depth of pallor, and how rapidly the pallor appears are
> evaluated. If pallor is not consistent, the test is read as negative.
> (See Op.Inst. 5.3.)

### 5.1.3 Cytology

**Summary.** Cytology covers the full transformation zone. Slide goes to
laboratory; cytologist reads per Bethesda 2014 (Annex 5). Two variants:
conventional PAP and liquid-based.

> **English translation:**
>
> *Basic concept.* Cytology is a laboratory method that fully includes the
> transformation zone of the cervix. The slide is briefly placed in a vial
> and either taken or shipped to the laboratory, where the cytologist examines
> it microscopically and produces a conclusion. The cytologist issues the
> conclusion per the international Bethesda classification (see Annex 5).
> Cytology is performed using either the **conventional PAP method** or the
> **liquid-based method**. (See Op.Inst. 5.4.)
>
> *Who is screened?* In administrative units lacking HPV testing capacity,
> during the preparatory period, women aged 30, 33, 36, 39, 42, 45, 48, 51,
> 54, 57, 60.
>
> *How is it performed?* The provider prepares the woman for exam (Op.Inst.
> 5.1), inserts speculum, visualizes the cervix, then with the cytology brush
> samples both the ecto- and endocervical regions. The smear is placed on a
> single slide, fixed in 96% alcohol solution, dried. The accompanying
> documentation is filled in carefully and the sample is sent to the laboratory.

## Section 5.2 — Diagnostic tests (triage) (pp. 50–51)

**Summary.** Three diagnostic tests, used to confirm and characterize lesions
identified by screening: colposcopy, tissue biopsy, and endocervical
curettage (ECC). All three are downstream of a positive screening result;
they are not screening tests themselves.

### 5.2.1 Colposcopy

> **English translation:**
>
> Colposcopy is a diagnostic test that magnifies the cervical epithelial cells
> and vasculature 5–15× under a strong light source. Used to confirm a
> positive screening result, choose the biopsy site, and decide whether
> treatment is needed. Requires dedicated equipment and trained providers.
> Is **not appropriate** for use as a screening test. (See Op.Inst. 5.6.)
> Recently, colposcopy systems with AI-powered diagnostics are in development
> in advanced countries.

L2 anchor: `parameters[id=diagnostic-tests].items` (colposcopy)

### 5.2.2 Tissue biopsy

> **English translation:**
>
> Tissue biopsy is performed under colposcopic guidance, taking samples from
> one or more abnormal areas of the cervix. Requires dedicated equipment and
> trained providers. Reveals cell-level changes within the cervix tissue. (See
> Op.Inst. 5.7.) Diagnoses normal, squamous-cell new alteration (CIN), and
> invasive cancer.

L2 anchor: `parameters[id=diagnostic-tests].items` (tissue-biopsy)

### 5.2.3 Endocervical curettage (ECC)

> **English translation:**
>
> ECC scrapes the cervical canal tissue with a small curette and the obtained
> tissue is placed in a fixing solution and sent to the laboratory.
> (See Op.Inst. 5.8.) ECC is performed in the following situations:
> - When the squamous-columnar junction is not visible
> - When glandular cell change is identified by cytology
> - When colposcopy gives an inadequate conclusion

L2 anchor: `parameters[id=diagnostic-tests].items` (endocervical-curettage)

### 5.2.4 Special diagnostic situations

> **English translation:**
>
> - When the transformation zone is not fully visible, take ECC. If this is
>   not possible, evaluate the screening result; if it could be a serious
>   lesion, perform LEEP or cervical excision surgery.
> - For pregnant women with positive screening, if cervical changes are
>   visible, perform colposcopy. Unless invasive cancer is suspected, schedule
>   tissue biopsy 6–12 weeks postpartum. If invasive cancer is suspected, refer
>   to a specialist centre.
> - For HIV-positive (ДОХ-той) patients with positive screening, follow the
>   same colposcopy and biopsy indications as other women — no modification
>   to the screening protocol is required because of HIV status.

### 5.2.5 Comparison of diagnostic tests — Хүснэгт 5.2 (p. 51)

> **English translation:**
>
> | Method | Strengths | Weaknesses |
> |---|---|---|
> | **Colposcopy** | Differentiates and diagnoses cervical pathology; identifies biopsy site | Requires specialist training and equipment; if procedure done directly may cause patient to be lost from monitoring |
> | **Tissue biopsy** | Confirms tissue-level lesion; differentiates ХУЭ vs. УХХХ | Requires specialist training and a dedicated pathology laboratory |
> | **ECC** | Used in cases where colposcopy is impossible because the SCJ is not visible at the ectocervix; identifies cervical changes | Requires specialist training and a dedicated pathology laboratory; if sample is fragmented, pathologist may have difficulty issuing conclusion |

## Section 5.4 — Pre-cancer treatment (pp. 56–60)

**Summary.** A/641 specifies **three treatment modalities** for pre-cancer:
LEEP, Thermal Ablation, and cone-shaped excision (cold knife). Treatment is
performed at aimag/district health centers or central facilities.

⚠️ **Currently missing from L2: Thermal Ablation** is not represented in
`parameters[id=diagnostic-tests].items`. It is a real third treatment modality
distinct from LEEP and cone biopsy.

### 5.4.1 Thermal Ablation (Дулаанаар түлэх эмчилгээ) — NEW

> **English translation:**
>
> Thermal Ablation heats the transformation zone to approximately 100°C with
> a metal probe, applied 5 times in 20–40 second cycles, in repeated
> applications. WHO-recommended approach. Trained providers can perform this
> at any tier of health facility.
>
> **Indications (Тавигдах шалгуур):**
> - CIN 2, 3 confirmed by tissue biopsy
> - Transformation zone fully visible at the ectocervix
> - Transformation zone in cervical canal but caudal margin visible OR fits in
>   tweezer tip
> - No invasive cancer or glandular cell dysplasia
> - No pelvic organ inflammation, vaginal trichomoniasis, candidal vaginosis,
>   "hairy fungus," genital sores, bleeding
> - 3 months postpartum (if recently given birth)
> - If hypertensive, after blood pressure has been treated
>
> **Post-procedure:** Healthy tissue regenerates in approximately 1 month;
> abstain from sex during this period; if necessary, advise the patient to use
> condoms.

### 5.4.2 LEEP (Цахилгаан гогцоогоор өөлөх эмчилгээ)

> **English translation:**
>
> LEEP (Loop Electrosurgical Excision Procedure) is a treatment that
> simultaneously cuts away the abnormal cervical tissue and seals the
> bleeding using electrosurgical loop. Used to treat all of the abnormal
> cervical tissue and the transformation zone. LEEP is suitable for
> diagnosis, treatment, and monitoring purposes. The procedure, including
> potential complications (bleeding), is performed by a trained ob-gyn at a
> health facility with anaesthesia capability, under local anaesthetic. The
> patient is monitored for a period after for bleeding risk. Detailed
> procedural notes in Op.Inst. 5.9.
>
> **Indications:**
> - CIN 1, 2, 3 confirmed by tissue biopsy
> - Even if lesion in cervical canal, the deep margin visible OR ≤ 1 cm deep
> - No invasive cancer or glandular cell dysplasia
> - No pelvic organ inflammation, vaginal infections, sores, bleeding
> - 3 months postpartum
> - If hypertensive, after BP treatment
>
> **Post-procedure:** Bleeding from vagina for first 7–10 days, lighter
> yellow discharge for one month afterward; abstain from sex during this
> period; if necessary, condom use.

### 5.4.3 Cone-shaped excision (Хутгаар шаантаг хэлбэртэй тайрах мэс засал)

> **English translation:**
>
> This is a procedure that excises the inner and outer portions of the cervix
> in a cone shape. Indication is when LEEP is not feasible. Specialist must
> perform; done in surgical theater. (See Op.Inst. 5.10.)
>
> **Post-procedure:** Yellow discharge from vagina for 7–14 days; tissue
> regenerates in 4–6 weeks; abstain from sex during this period.

## Translator's notes (Claude, May 2026)

1. **AIS / БЭБӨ as a distinct lesion category.** A/641 treats AIS
   (Adenocarcinoma in situ; "Булчирхайлаг эсийн байран өмөн" = БЭБӨ) as a
   separate diagnostic entity from CIN, with its own treatment branch
   (Зураг 5.10). The current L2's `axes.cin_grade` enum collapses this — AIS
   is glandular, not squamous. Worth adding as a sibling axis.

2. **Thermal Ablation as a third treatment modality.** The L2's current
   `parameters[id=diagnostic-tests].items` lists colposcopy, biopsy, ECC,
   and LEEP, but is silent on Thermal Ablation. The source treats it as a
   peer to LEEP for CIN 2/3 with similar indications. The naming of the L2
   parameter (`diagnostic-tests`) is also itself confusing — LEEP and Thermal
   Ablation are *treatment* modalities, not diagnostic tests. Worth
   restructuring.

3. **AGC sub-algorithm.** The current L2 enumerates AGC in
   `axes.cytology_result` but does not define a `triage_sub_algorithm` for
   it. The source algorithm (Зураг 5.7) has the most distinctive
   structure of any sub-algorithm — it specifically calls out 35+ with
   abnormal uterine bleeding for additional endometrial biopsy. Worth adding.

4. **Three CIN-confirmed sub-algorithms missing from L2.** Зураг 5.8 (CIN1
   confirmed), 5.9 (CIN2 confirmed), and 5.10 (CIN2/3 or AIS treatment) all
   define follow-up management algorithms that are post-diagnosis, not
   triage. The current L2 stops at the triage level. If post-diagnosis
   PlanDefinitions are in scope for cxca, these algorithms need L2
   representation.

5. **Post-treatment monitoring intervals.** The 6/12/24-month surveillance
   schedule and the 5-year HSIL post-treatment annual cycle are concrete
   timing rules that would translate to CQL `OverdueFor` defines in a future
   post-treatment PlanDefinition. Currently no L2 anchor.

6. **Хүснэгт 5.1 (screening test comparison) and 5.4 (complications) are
   narrative content, not algorithm content.** I included them because they
   are useful for SME critique even if not L2-relevant. They could be moved
   to the L1 README or a separate `narrative-tables.md` file if the L1
   per-chapter files become too long.
